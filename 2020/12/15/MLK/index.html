MLK

Posted on 2020-12-15

As you continue reading (and perhaps listening to the speech), pay attention to the figures of speech used by King to depict states of oppression like slavery and states of freedom. Write down at least 3 such literary devices here, with a short explanation of what they symbolize/mean. Pay especially to the way in which metaphors or light, fire, heat, and water, coolness are used in relation to justice and injustice:

  1. Parallelism ( In paragraph 6, MLK use Now is the time……….Now is the time…..Now is the time to show the importance of the freedon, democracy and qualty.

2.metaphor( MLK use great beacon light to metaphor the Emancipation proclamation in the beginning of the text, and flames of withering injustice means
Negro slaves who had been seared.

  1. repeat /.（ we will be able to work together, to pray together, to struggle together, to go to jail together, to stand up for freedom together, knowing that we will be free one day.）MLK use together several time in same paragraph to let all of the the negro to know that they will fight together for the same goal.
  1. MLK speaks of America as having given its Black citizens a “bad check.” A bad check is a check one writes to cover a payment for which there are no funds. What does he mean by a “bad check” here?

The bad check means that The negro was not endowed with the rights of life, freedom and pursuit of happiness

Where does the audience’s reaction show that MLK’s speech is especially effective? Where do you hear applause? Pause in one of such moments and try to analyze what in MLK’s speech the audience is reacting to. Which words seem to strike a chord (create an emotion in the crowd listening to the speech). Write down the line that caused this emotion, and write 3-4 sentences of analysis why it was effective. What emotion, do you think, do people have at that moment.

Martin Luther King’s speech is characterized by its ability to combine personal views, audience emotions and language. His speech is skillful in language and skillful in rhetoric. In addition, his ability to mobilize the atmosphere of the scene is great. In his speech, there is a kind of magic power that directly points to the people’s heart, which often makes the crowd excited

How does the speech work as a speech of persuading, motivating, and moving people to action? What role does empathy play in this?

he success of MLK’s speech is because at that time was largely in line with the historical background that negros were oppressed by racial discrimination and urgently needed to be understand and treated fairly
